The problem

Special education documents are often long, technical, and emotionally loaded. Families are expected to interpret legal language, assessment data, and goal progress—usually without training, time, or support.

  • IEPs commonly span dozens of pages with specialized terminology and compliance requirements.
  • Families with fewer resources may face a “two-tier system” where expert help is expensive and unevenly distributed.
  • Immigrant families can face an additional barrier when documents and meetings are English-first.
Why we built Autism Advisor
When our aunt spent $1,500 on an education advocate just to understand our cousin’s 100-page education plan, we knew technology could democratize advocate-level support. This story is shared with family permission. We do not share any identifying student information.

The solution

Autism Advisor combines generative AI with proprietary, state-specific knowledge graphs to produce understandable, actionable summaries—without relying on generic “best guesses.”

How it works (high level)

  • Upload a special education plan (IEP/PDF).
  • Extract structured text with Apple PDFKit (preserves formatting and sections).
  • Analyze with an AI engine (LLM + state-specific legal knowledge graphs).
  • Deliver visual summaries, progress insights, and plain-language Q&A.

What users get

  • Visual summaries of strengths, needs, goals, and services.
  • Progress analysis to spot gaps between goals and performance.
  • Plain-language assistant for questions like “What accommodations should I request?”
  • Accessibility-first UX with speech-to-text and text-to-speech support.
  • Multilingual support for families who need guidance beyond English.
What makes this different
Generic AI can summarize. Autism Advisor is designed to be state-grounded so families receive accurate, context-aware information aligned to local requirements. This is an informational support tool and does not provide legal advice.

Traction & validation

  • Piloted with 10 families across Orange County (using publicly available redacted documents for privacy).
  • Published on the iOS App Store (after strengthening privacy and account management flows).
  • Validated with feedback from caregivers and professionals in the autism ecosystem.

We do not store or publish identifiable student data. Demonstrations use redacted or sample documents.

What’s next

  • Expand state coverage and continue improving state-specific grounding.
  • Scale multilingual support to additional languages as we grow nationally.
  • Develop a web experience for use during meetings and professional workflows.
  • Pursue pilots and partnerships with districts, clinics, and advocacy organizations.
